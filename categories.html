Diversity of Turtle Species

Green Turtle

The Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas) is a species of sea turtle that is found in Sri Lanka. Green Turtles are named for the greenish color of their fat, which comes from their herbivorous diet of seagrasses and algae. They are one of the largest species of sea turtles, with adults growing up to 5 feet in length and weighing over 500 pounds. Green Turtles can be found nesting on several beaches in Sri Lanka, including Rekawa, Kosgoda, and Pottuvil. These beaches are important nesting sites for this species, and conservation efforts are in place to protect them. Green Turtles face threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and the harvesting of eggs and meat for consumption.

Hawksbill Turtle

The Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata) is another species of sea turtle that can be found in Sri Lanka. Hawksbill Turtles are known for their distinctive pointed beaks and colorful, patterned shells. They are listed as critically endangered by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) due to their vulnerability to habitat loss, pollution, and overexploitation for their meat, eggs, and shells. Hawksbill Turtles can be found nesting on several beaches in Sri Lanka, including Rumassala, Hikkaduwa, and Unawatuna. These beaches are important nesting sites for this species, and conservation efforts are in place to protect them.

Olive Ridley Turtle

The Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea) is another species of sea turtle found in Sri Lanka. It is the smallest and most abundant of the sea turtles that nest on Sri Lanka's beaches. Olive Ridley Turtles are named for their olive-colored carapace (shell) and are known for their synchronized nesting behavior, called arribadas. Olive Ridley Turtles can be found nesting on several beaches in Sri Lanka, including Rekawa, Kosgoda, and Pottuvil. These beaches are important nesting sites for this species, and conservation efforts are in place to protect them. Like other sea turtle species, Olive Ridley Turtles face threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and the harvesting of eggs and meat for consumption.

Loggerhead Turtle

The Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta) is one of the five species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka. It is a critically endangered species that is protected under the Fauna and Flora Protection Ordinance in Sri Lanka. The Loggerhead Turtle is named for its large, blocky head and strong jaw, which it uses to feed on hard-shelled prey such as crabs and mollusks. Adults can grow up to three feet in length and weigh up to 350 pounds.

Leatherback Turtle

The Leatherback Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) is the largest of all sea turtle species and is found in all major oceans around the world. In Sri Lanka, the Leatherback Turtle is one of five species of sea turtles that nest along the country's beaches. In this essay, we will discuss the physical characteristics, habitat, behavior, and conservation status of the Leatherback Turtle in Sri Lanka. Leatherback Turtles have a unique appearance, with a flexible, leathery shell covered in small, white bumps.
